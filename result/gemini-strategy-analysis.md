## 1. THE GOAL & PHILOSOPHY

**The Goal:** To achieve exceptional compounding (targeting >30% CAGR) by identifying quality businesses that are temporarily distressed, misunderstood, or cyclically depressed.

**The Philosophy:**
- **Contrarian Aggression:** The strategy only engages when a stock has crashed (-60% from ATH). It assumes the market overreacts to short-term fear.
- **Asymmetry:** Upside must be >100% (doubling potential), while downside is mathematically capped by solvency checks.
- **Concentration:** "Diworsification" is rejected. The portfolio holds a maximum of 12 stocks, allowing for high conviction (up to 20% initial position sizing).
- **Turnaround Focused:** "The stock and the business are not the same thing." The strategy profits from the gap between current sentiment (stock price) and business reality (intrinsic value).

**Target Investor:** An active, intermediate-to-advanced investor with high emotional fortitude, capable of holding volatile positions for 1-4 years while ignoring market noise.

## 2. THE STRATEGY (The 8-Step Lifecycle)

The scanner operates as a funnel. A stock must survive every step to enter the portfolio.

### Step 1: The Signal (The "Broken Chart")
The entry ticket is a 60% drop from all-time highs. This filters out 90% of the market to focus solely on distressed assets.
- *Quick Sniff:* Is the industry understandable? Is it dying (secular decline)?

### Step 2: The First Filter (The Survival Check)
A 5-point rapid audit to ensure the company won't go bankrupt before the turnaround.
- *Key Checks:* Solvency (cash vs. debt), Dilution (is SBC eating value?), ROIC (>6% min), and Valuation (must show 30% CAGR potential).

### Step 3: Competitor Comparison
The "Relative Value" test. The target is compared against industry peers.
- *Goal:* Ensure we aren't buying the "cheap" player that is losing market share to a "better" player. Balance sheet strength is prioritized over margins here.

### Step 4: Qualitative Deep Dive
The "Story" check.
- *Mandatory:* Must identify a specific **Catalyst**. If there is no catalyst to change the narrative, the stock is a "value trap" and is rejected.
- *Management:* Assessment of CEO track record and compensation incentives (FCF alignment is preferred).

### Step 5: Valuation
Determining Fair Value.
- Uses a simplified 4-input model (Revenue x Margin x Multiple / Shares) to determine if the 30% CAGR hurdle is met.

### Step 6: Decision Scoring
Quantifying the risk/reward. (See Section 3).

### Step 7: Position Sizing
Allocating capital based on the score. (See Section 3).

### Step 8: Monitoring & Exit
- *Sell Triggers:* Target price hit (returns drop <30%), rapid appreciation (returns drop <15%), or thesis break.
- *Hold:* Price drops are ignored if the business thesis remains intact.

## 3. THE SCORING SYSTEM (The Quantified Brain)

Decisions are not made on "gut feel" but on a weighted scoring model.

### The Mathematics of Conviction
The scoring formula favors **capital preservation** over moonshot potential.

- **Downside Risk (45-50% Weight):** The most critical metric.
  - *The Penalty Curve:* Downside is penalized non-linearly. A 30% downside risk is penalized at `30 * 1.15`, while a 60% downside is penalized at `60 * 1.30`. This makes high-risk stocks mathematically impossible to buy heavily.
- **Probability (35-40% Weight):** How likely is the base case?
- **CAGR (15% Weight):** Surprisingly, the upside magnitude has the *lowest* weight. The strategy assumes if you protect the downside and pick high probability setups, the upside takes care of itself.

### Scoring Formula

```
adjusted_downside = downside_pct * (1 + (downside_pct / 100) * 0.5)
Score = (100 - adjusted_downside) * 0.45 + probability * 0.40 + min(cagr, 100) * 0.15
```

### Position Sizing Matrix
Allocation is dictated by the score, not intuition.

| Score Range | Max Position Size | Interpretation |
|-------------|-------------------|----------------|
| 75+ | 15-20% | Highest conviction — exceptional risk/reward |
| 65-74 | 10-15% | Strong opportunity — clear thesis with manageable risk |
| 55-64 | 6-10% | Good opportunity — some uncertainty but favorable asymmetry |
| 45-54 | 3-6% | Moderate — acceptable but meaningful questions remain |
| Below 45 | 0% (Watchlist) | Insufficient score — do not invest |

### Hard Breakpoints (The "Kill Switch")
- **CAGR < 30%:** Size = 0%.
- **Probability < 60%:** Size = 0%.
- **Downside Risk 80-99%:** Size capped at 5%.
- **Downside Risk 100%:** Size capped at 3% (even if upside is massive).

## 5. THE VALUATION MODEL

The model rejects complexity in favor of "obviousness."

### The Formula

```
Target Price = Revenue x FCF Margin x FCF Multiple / Shares Outstanding
```

### CAGR Calculation

```
CAGR = ((target_price / current_price) ^ (1 / years)) - 1
```

### Industry Multiple Baselines

| Industry Type | Baseline FCF Multiple | Notes |
|---------------|----------------------|-------|
| Cyclical/Industrials | 12-15x | Lower due to downturn risk |
| Consumer Staples | 25-28x | Durable brands, stable cash flows |
| Healthcare | Higher than cyclicals | Regulation, development timelines |
| China/Geopolitical | Apply discount | e.g., 18x instead of low 20s |

### Multiple Adjustment Factors

**Adjust UP for:**
- Above-average ROIC/ROCE + durable competitive advantages (moats)
- Faster growth than industry peers
- Improving fundamentals (margin expansion, deleveraging)

**Adjust DOWN for:**
- Above-average leverage relative to peers or own history
- Elevated risks (regulatory, geopolitical, cyclical)
- Deteriorating fundamentals (declining margins, rising debt, losing market share)

### The "Heroic Assumptions" Check
A valuation fails if it requires:
- Revenue growth required is 3x the industry average
- FCF margin estimate exceeds the company's 10-year peak
- FCF multiple exceeds the company's historical median by more than 50%

### The Hurdle
The investment *must* project a **30% Annualized Return (CAGR)**.
- *Exception:* 20% CAGR is allowed only for top-tier CEOs with a 6+ year runway.

| S&P 500 down 15% | 25% |

*Logic:* Buy more aggressively when the general market is fearful.

### Capital Deployment Scenarios

**Scenario 1: Cash Available**
- Compare new idea's score to best existing position that still has room to add
- Higher score gets the capital

**Scenario 2: No Cash Available**
1. Does any current position have forward returns below 15%/year? If yes AND new position has lower downside risk → sell for new idea
2. If no position below 15% → need 40+ point score gap to justify swap

**Scenario 3: No Cash + Already Maxed on Theme**
- Compare new idea to weakest holding within same theme only
- Apply Scenario 2 rules

### Sell Triggers
| Trigger | Condition |
|---------|-----------|
| Target reached | Forward returns fall below 30% hurdle |
| Rapid move | Forward returns fall below 10-15%/year |
| Thesis broken | Fundamental business change (not price change) |

### What is NOT a Sell Trigger
- Stock price going down
- Scary headlines without business impact
- Fear or general market sentiment

## 8. STRENGTHS & POTENTIAL BLIND SPOTS

### Strengths
1. **Discipline:** The mathematical scoring system removes emotion. The "non-linear downside penalty" is a sophisticated risk management tool.
2. **Asymmetry:** By demanding 100% upside and checking solvency, the strategy targets "Heads I win big, Tails I don't lose much."
3. **Avoids Value Traps:** The mandatory "Catalyst" check (Step 4, Q5) and "Growth/Solvency" checks prevent buying stocks that are cheap for a good reason.
4. **Counter-cyclical Leverage:** Increasing leverage during broad market declines is a mathematically sound approach when combined with careful individual stock selection.
5. **Clear Decision Framework:** Every step is codified, reducing ad-hoc decisions and cognitive bias.

### Blind Spots / Weaknesses
1. **"Quality" Exclusion:** The 60% drawdown rule automatically excludes high-quality compounders that rarely crash (e.g., Costco, Visa). The strategy will miss almost all "steady winners."
2. **Timing Risk:** "Turnarounds seldom turn." Beaten-down stocks can stay dead money for years (opportunity cost). The strategy requires significant patience.
3. **Concentration Volatility:** With 34% in China and 33% in Auto Parts, a regulatory change in China or a structural shift in auto repair could devastate >30% of the portfolio instantly.
4. **Complexity:** Requires deep knowledge of accounting (ROIC, Dilution, Debt Schedules) that goes beyond simple P/E ratios. The scanner automates screening but qualitative analysis still requires significant expertise.
5. **Market Environment Sensitivity:** The strategy thrives in mean-reverting markets but can significantly underperform in momentum-driven, growth-led bull markets (e.g., 2020-2024 tech rally).
6. **Small Universe:** The 60%+ drawdown + excluded industries + 5-check filter may reduce the investable universe to very few candidates in certain market environments.

### Favorable Market Conditions
- Broad market corrections (more broken charts to choose from)
- Rising interest rates (value stocks outperform)
- Sector rotations out of growth into value
- Economic recovery phases (turnarounds materialize)

### Unfavorable Market Conditions
- Extended growth/momentum rallies (opportunity cost of holding turnarounds)
- Sustained deflation (turnarounds delayed)
- Sector-wide structural shifts (entire industries can be disrupted)

## 9. KEY HARD RULES (Non-Negotiables)

These rules must NEVER be bypassed under any circumstances:

1. **Entry Trigger:** Stock must be down **60%+ from All-Time Highs**
2. **The Hurdle:** Expected return must be **30%+ CAGR** (Exception: 20%+ with top-tier CEO + 6yr+ runway)
3. **The Catalyst Rule:** No catalyst identified = **Automatic Pass** (HARD RULE)
4. **Industry Exclusions:** Never invest in the 14 excluded industries (Airlines, Banks, Biotech, Pure AI, etc.)
5. **Dilution Kill:** Never invest if the company is issuing shares to pay off debt
6. **Max Positions:** Never exceed 12 portfolio positions
7. **Theme Concentration:** Never exceed 50% in a single catalyst/theme
8. **Solvency Gate:** If solvency risk exists and is not fully priced in, **Fail**
9. **Position Size Zero:** CAGR < 30% OR Probability < 60% = position size is 0% (no investment)
10. **Score Minimum:** Score below 45 = do not invest (watchlist only)
11. **Downside Caps:** 80-99% downside = max 5% position; 100% downside = max 3% position
12. **Heroic Assumptions:** Reject any valuation requiring margins above 10yr peak, growth 3x industry, or multiples 50%+ above historical median
