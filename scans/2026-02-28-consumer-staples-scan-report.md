### BRBR — **DEMOTED** (Triggers a + d)

**10-K Filing Date:** 2025-11-18 | **Risk Factors Reviewed:** 100

**Trigger (a): Customer concentration >70%**
- Walmart/Sam's Club + Costco + Amazon = **74.0% of net sales** (FY2025 ended Sep 30, 2025)
- FY2024: 74.8% | FY2023: 75.3% — consistently above the 70% threshold across 3 years
- This is structural, not temporary. Three accounts control three-quarters of revenue. Loss of any single account would be catastrophic and is explicitly flagged by management as a material risk.
- This risk was NOT identified in the pre-enrichment analysis.

**Trigger (d): Supply chain SPOF threatening an identified catalyst**
- BRBR discloses: *"We utilize a sole supplier for the aseptic packaging for, and our third-party contract manufacturers use equipment from the same sole supplier to manufacture, our Premier Protein RTD shakes in the 11 ounce size."*
- Premier Protein RTD shakes = **81.7% of net sales** (FY2025). The entire business depends on a single packaging supplier.
- This directly threatens Catalyst #1 (destocking recovery): if the sole packaging supplier experiences disruption, BellRing cannot scale production back up to meet recovered demand. The recovery thesis assumes manufacturing flexibility that a sole-source dependency fundamentally constrains.

**Additional notable risks (not demotion triggers):**
- Revenue concentration: RTD protein shakes = 81.7% of net sales — single product category dependency
- GLP-1/weight loss medication risk: *"The growing acceptance and use of medication to manage weight could negatively affect the demand for many types of food in general, including our products."* — disclosed as a risk to protein demand
- Post Holdings governance overlap: overlapping directors and management with Post creates potential conflicts of interest

**Demotion justification:** Two independent demotion triggers fire. Customer concentration exceeds 70% across all three reported years, and the sole-source packaging supplier creates a SPOF that directly threatens the destocking recovery catalyst (the primary thesis). Score remains 52.90 but BRBR is ranked below all non-demoted candidates.

### COTY — **DEMOTED** (Trigger c)

**10-K Filing Date:** 2024-08-20 | **Risk Factors Reviewed:** 64

**Trigger (c): Previously unidentified kill-level risk without disclosed mitigation**
- COTY discloses: *"We are involved in numerous lawsuits involving product liability issues, most involving allegations related to alleged asbestos in our talc-based cosmetic products, allegedly leading to mesothelioma."*
- This is a **talc/asbestos litigation risk** — the same category of litigation that drove Johnson & Johnson to pursue a bankruptcy maneuver and Revlon into restructuring. The word "numerous" indicates material volume.
- COTY states only that they "believe that we have valid defenses" but provides no quantification of potential liability, no reserve disclosure, and no specific mitigation plan.
- This risk was **NOT identified in the pre-enrichment analysis**. The scan report discusses debt burden, interim CEO, and consumer beauty challenges but makes zero mention of asbestos litigation.
- For a company already carrying $4.2B in debt at 3.7x leverage, an unquantified asbestos litigation liability represents a potential equity-destroying event with no visible management mitigation plan.

**Additional notable risks (not demotion triggers):**
- Brand licensing dependency: Coty licenses trademarks for many product lines (Burberry, Hugo Boss, Gucci, etc.) with minimum sales level obligations — license termination risk if turnaround stalls
- JABH controlling shareholder: >50% voting power makes Coty a "controlled company" exempt from certain NYSE governance requirements — limits minority shareholder protections
- 71% non-US revenue exposure with significant FX risk (euro, pound, yuan)

**Demotion justification:** Talc/asbestos litigation represents a previously unidentified kill-level legal risk with no disclosed management mitigation plan beyond asserting "valid defenses." Given COTY's existing 3.7x leverage and negative equity trajectory, an adverse litigation outcome could be equity-destroying. Score remains 47.44 but COTY is ranked below all non-demoted candidates.
