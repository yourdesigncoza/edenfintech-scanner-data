- **Moats:** Moderate -- #1 brand position in growing RTD protein category, distribution scale, manufacturing relationships. But low barriers to entry in protein drinks; competition intensifying from club store private label and new entrants.
- **Catalysts:**
  1. Continued household penetration expansion in RTD protein (still early innings vs. addressable market)
  2. Innovation pipeline: new formats, flavors, international expansion potential
  3. Aggressive share repurchases reducing float (shares down from 135M to 127M in 2 years)
  4. Operating leverage as revenue scales against relatively fixed cost base
- **Management:** Adequate -- Darcy Davenport has led strong execution since IPO. Company consistently meets or beats guidance. Compensation tied to Adjusted EBITDA growth.
- **Suggested size:** 6-8% | **Confidence flags:** solvency_borderline (negative equity from spinoff leverage, $1.1B debt), valuation_subjective (multiple constrained by leverage), cyclical_timing (protein trend durability uncertain long-term)

### 3. SAM (Boston Beer Company) -- Score: 63.3 | CAGR: 39% | Downside: 30%
- **Thesis:** Founder Jim Koch returned as CEO in August 2025, bringing founder-operator DNA back to a company that lost its way during the hard seltzer boom and bust. Gross margins are recovering (38.8% in 2021 to 45.6% in 2025 with 410bps expansion). The balance sheet is a fortress ($223M cash, virtually no debt) and the company is aggressively buying back stock ($200M+ per year). Sun Cruiser (iced tea + spirits RTD) grew 300%+ and became a top-5 RTD spirits brand. At 11.5x P/FCF, this is extremely cheap for a consumer staples company.
- **Valuation:** Revenue $2.1B -> $2.3B by 2029 (3% CAGR, conservative given flat volumes + pricing) x 12% FCF margin (continued recovery from current 10.3%, historically ~15% operating margin pre-crash) x 22x FCF multiple (consumer staples discount from 25-28x baseline due to volume declines) / 10.0M shares (continued aggressive buybacks from 10.6M) = $607 per share (39% CAGR over 3 years)
- **Worst case:** $160 (30% loss) -- Revenue declines to $2.0B, margins stall at 7% FCF, market assigns only 12x multiple for lack of growth, 10.5M shares
- **Moats:** Moderate -- Sam Adams and Twisted Tea have strong brand recognition. Twisted Tea is the #1 flavored malt beverage. Sun Cruiser is a breakout brand. But craft beer is crowded and alcohol consumption is in secular decline among younger demographics.
- **Catalysts:**
  1. Sun Cruiser scaling rapidly (300%+ volume growth, revenue and margin accretive)
  2. Gross margin recovery program: procurement savings, brewery efficiency, waste reduction (targeting 48-50% gross margin in 2026)
  3. Founder-CEO return: Jim Koch back with 18.5% ownership stake, aligned incentives
  4. Aggressive buybacks: $200M+/year with $215M remaining authorization on $1.6B total program
  5. Price increases of 1-2% in 2026
- **Management:** Strong -- Jim Koch (founder, 18.5% owner) returned as CEO in Aug 2025. His skin-in-the-game is enormous. Historical track record of building Boston Beer from a startup to a multi-billion dollar company. The return of the founder after a period of professional management stumbles is a classic turnaround catalyst.
- **Suggested size:** 8% | **Confidence flags:** catalyst_timing_unclear (volume declines may persist despite innovation), qualitative_uncertain (secular alcohol decline headwind), valuation_subjective (appropriate multiple debatable given volume trends)

**10-K Risk Factors** (via Massive.com):
- Key risks (37 factors, filing date 2026-02-24):
  1. **Accelerating volume declines:** Depletion volume declined -5% (2022), -6% (2023), -2% (2024), -4% (2025). The 2025 acceleration reverses the improvement seen in 2024. The US beer market has declined for 10 consecutive years with 2025 declines accelerating to ~4%. This is the central risk to the thesis -- margin recovery without volume recovery is structurally harder.
  2. **Generational alcohol headwind:** "Younger generations including Millennials and Gen Z are embracing moderation or abstinence, influenced by wellness trends and the rise of non-alcoholic alternatives." The 'sober curious' movement is explicitly cited. This is a demographic shift, not a cyclical downturn.
  3. **Major beverage company entry into alcohol:** Coke, Pepsi, Monster, and Arizona have entered alcoholic beverages through licensing/partnerships. Sun Cruiser's RTD spirits category will face competition from vastly better-funded entrants.
- Catalyst conflicts: Sun Cruiser scaling may be constrained by brewery capacity -- "Pennsylvania Brewery operating at or close to current capacity in peak months." Large brewer influence on distributors could slow distribution expansion -- many of SAM's 300+ distributors depend on AB InBev or Molson Coors for most of their revenue and may be pressured to deprioritize SAM brands.
- Unaddressed: Dual-class share structure gives Jim Koch 100% of Class B voting rights and control over majority of board elections. Class A shareholders have minimal governance recourse if turnaround strategy fails. While Koch's 18.5% ownership is aligned, the lack of external governance check is a risk if founder's judgment proves wrong.
- **FLAG FOR MANUAL REVIEW:** Brewery capacity constraints at Pennsylvania facility during peak months were not in the original analysis. If Sun Cruiser scales as projected (catalyst #1), capacity could become a bottleneck. Verify whether capex plans address this.
