Regional bank failures over the last 40 years (1986–2026) often stemmed from real estate exposure, poor risk management, and liquidity strains, with patterns repeating across crises like the S&L debacle, early 1990s recession, 2008 GFC, and 2023 turmoil. [en.wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_largest_bank_failures_in_the_United_States)

### Key Examples
| Bank | Year | Category | Gradual/Binary | Warning Signs | Outcome |
|------|------|----------|----------------|---------------|---------|
| Continental Illinois National Bank (Chicago, regional/major) | 1984* | Liquidity | Binary (run after rumors) | High exposure to risky Penn Square oil/gas loans; ignored internal warnings on credit problems; reliance on unstable wholesale funding (only 15% insured deposits)  [federalreservehistory](https://www.federalreservehistory.org/essays/continental-illinois) | Bailed out by FDIC/Fed ($7.5B+ support); nationalized temporarily; sold to Bank of America in 1994  [federalreservehistory](https://www.federalreservehistory.org/essays/continental-illinois) |
| Lincoln Savings and Loan (Irvine, CA, regional S&L) | 1989 | Fraud/Regulatory | Gradual | Regulatory violations found in audits; illegal cash transfers to parent company; junk bonds and risky investments amid deregulation  [en.wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Charles_Keating) | Seized by regulators; $3.4B taxpayer cost via RTC; Keating convicted of fraud  [en.wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Charles_Keating) |
| BankUnited FSB (Coral Gables, FL, regional thrift) | 2009 | Liquidity/Operational | Gradual | Heavy concentration in Florida adjustable-rate mortgages; rising non-performing loans ($1.8B); inadequate capital for losses  [oig.treasury](https://oig.treasury.gov/system/files/Documents/OIG10042%20(BankUnited%20MLR).pdf) | FDIC-seized; sold to private investors; $4.5B+ material loss  [oig.treasury](https://oig.treasury.gov/system/files/Documents/OIG10042%20(BankUnited%20MLR).pdf) |
| Guaranty Bank (Austin, TX, regional) | 2009 | Liquidity | Gradual | Overexposure to commercial real estate; loan losses eroded capital during recession  [en.wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_largest_bank_failures_in_the_United_States) | Failed and absorbed by BBVA Compass  [en.wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_largest_bank_failures_in_the_United_States) |
| Silicon Valley Bank (Santa Clara, CA, regional tech-focused) | 2023 | Liquidity | Binary (accelerated run) | Unhedged long-term bond losses from rate hikes; rapid uninsured deposit growth; deposit outflows intensified ($42B in days)  [oversight.house](https://oversight.house.gov/wp-content/uploads/2023/05/GAO-Bank-Failures-Testimony.pdf) | FDIC closed; assets sold to First Citizens  [investopedia](https://www.investopedia.com/bank-failures-eclipse-2008-7487280) |
| Signature Bank (New York, NY, regional) | 2023 | Operational/Liquidity | Binary | Poor governance/risk management; overreliance on uninsured deposits (esp. crypto); ignored examiner concerns  [oversight.house](https://oversight.house.gov/wp-content/uploads/2023/05/GAO-Bank-Failures-Testimony.pdf) | FDIC closed; assets transferred to Flagstar/others  [investopedia](https://www.investopedia.com/bank-failures-eclipse-2008-7487280) |
| First Republic Bank (San Francisco, CA, regional) | 2023 | Contagion/Liquidity | Binary | Uninsured deposit run post-SVB/Signature; interest rate risk in loans; depositor confidence loss  [fdic](https://www.fdic.gov/news/press-releases/2023/pr23073a.pdf) | Sold to JPMorgan Chase; $13B+ FDIC loss  [fdic](https://www.fdic.gov/news/press-releases/2023/pr23073a.pdf) |

*Included as pivotal early example influencing patterns. S&L failures (e.g., 1,000+ in 1980s) were mostly gradual from deregulation, fraud, and real estate volatility. 2008–2010 saw 100s of regional failures from similar operational/liquidity issues in CRE. Recent cases highlight binary runs via social media on uninsured funds. [burrowscap](https://www.burrowscap.com/blog/echoes-of-the-past-the-banking-and-sl-crisis-of-the-1980s)
