## Wells Fargo — Regulatory Turnaround (2018-2025)
- Distress trigger: fake accounts scandal (2016), subsequent CFPB enforcement, Fed asset cap ($1.95T) imposed Feb 2018
- Cap persisted 7 years; 13 consent orders closed between 2019-2025
- New CEO Charles Scharf installed Oct 2019 — sweeping operational overhaul
- Asset cap lifted by Federal Reserve in 2025 (exact date: confirmed Feb 2025 per newsroom.wf.com)
- Sources: https://newsroom.wf.com/news-releases/news-details/2025/Wells-Fargo-Confirms-that-the-Federal-Reserve-Has-Removed-the-Limits-on-Growth-in-Total-Assets-Imposed-in-its-2018-Consent-Order-and-Announces-a-Special-Employee-Award/default.aspx

## Citigroup — Post-GFC Restructuring (2008-2013)
- Distress trigger: $130B losses on loans/investments 2007-2009; TARP $25B (Oct 2008) + $20B additional + $306B asset guarantee
- Stock hit single digits Nov 2008 (first time since 1996)
- Government held ~27% equity stake via TARP conversion
- Full TARP repayment Dec 2010; government exited equity position by Dec 2010
- Organizational restructure: divested non-core assets (Primerica, Smith Barney, etc.)
- ROA recovered to ~0.8% by 2013
- Sources: https://elischolar.library.yale.edu/cgi/viewcontent.cgi?article=1592&context=journal-of-financial-crises

## Pattern
- Regulatory-triggered distress (not credit losses alone) requires CEO change + operational restructuring
- Full recovery timeline: 5-7 years for regulatory consent order resolution
- TARP/government injection cases: equity recovery took 3-5 years post-repayment
