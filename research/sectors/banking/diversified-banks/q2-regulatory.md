## Primary Regulators
- Federal Reserve: primary supervisor of BHCs (Bank Holding Companies)
- OCC (Office of the Comptroller of the Currency): national banks
- FDIC: deposit insurance, resolution authority
- SEC / FINRA: securities activities
- CFPB: consumer protection

## GSIB Framework
- US G-SIBs (2025 FSB list): JPMorgan Chase, Bank of America, Wells Fargo, Citigroup, Goldman Sachs, Morgan Stanley, BNY Mellon, State Street
- GSIB surcharge: 1-3.5% CET1 additional buffer depending on systemic score
- Enhanced Supplementary Leverage Ratio (eSLR): final rule effective April 1, 2026
  - New eSLR buffer = 50% of GSIB Method 1 surcharge (replacing fixed 2%)
  - Sources: https://www.federalreserve.gov/newsevents/pressreleases/bcreg20251125b.htm

## Capital Requirements (Large Banks)
- Minimum CET1: 4.5% of RWA
- Stress Capital Buffer (SCB): minimum 2.5%, determined by DFAST
- GSIB surcharge: variable 1-3.5%
- Effective total CET1 target: 11.5-16% for GSIBs

## Basel III Endgame
- 2023 proposal would have required ~16% capital increase for large banks
- Final rule expected early 2026: ~9% increase, capital-neutral for most banks under $250B
- Source: https://blog.freshfields.us/post/102lymd/2025-bank-regulatory-roundup-and-what-to-look-for-in-2026

## Enforcement History
- Wells Fargo: $3.7B CFPB fine (Dec 2022), asset cap 2018-2025 (lifted 2025)
- Citigroup: $75M SEC fine (subprime disclosure); 2015 currency manipulation guilty plea
- JPMorgan: London Whale $920M settlement (2013)
