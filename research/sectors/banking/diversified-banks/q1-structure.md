# Q1: Diversified Banks — Structure & Revenue Model
# Source: WebSearch fallback (Perplexity MCP unavailable)
# Date: 2026-03-02

## Revenue Model
- Net Interest Income (NII): spread between cost of deposits/borrowings and loan/securities yields
- Non-interest income: trading, investment banking, wealth management, fees, card revenues
- Diversified banks earn ~40-50% of revenue from non-NII sources (vs ~20% for regionals)
- Industry NIM: 3.22% full-year 2024 (FDIC), 8bps lower than 2023
- Q4 2024 ROA: 1.11% industry-wide; full-year 2024 net income: $268.2B (+$14.1B YoY)

## Capital Intensity
- Tier 1 capital held as buffer against risk-weighted assets
- Industry aggregate CET1 ~13% for large banks (H1 2025, Fed Supervision Report)
- Return on equity: varies 10-15% for healthy diversified banks

## Competitive Dynamics
- Big 4 (JPM, BAC, WFC, C) have economies of scale advantages in technology and compliance
- Cross-subsidy between business lines (investment banking can offset NIM compression periods)
- Barriers: regulatory capital, compliance infrastructure, brand trust, deposit network
