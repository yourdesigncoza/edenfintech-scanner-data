## PCS Mapping

### Q1: Is risk primarily operational/modelable?
**Yes** — Core risks (credit, interest rate) are among the most mathematically mature in finance. Bank inputs (yield curves, duration, LTV) and outputs (NIM, NCOs) can be modeled with high precision using GAAP/regulatory data. The S&L crisis and SVB collapse were both modelable duration mismatches.

### Q2: Is regulatory discretion minimal?
**No** — While capital requirements (Tier 1) are rigid, the response to distress is highly discretionary and political. Whether a bank gets a "Systemic Risk Exception" vs standard failure is subjective. In 2023, SVB and Signature got exceptions; WaMu in 2008 did not. Rules change based on political climate and contagion speed.

### Q3: Are there strong historical precedents for turnarounds?
**No (qualified)** — Once a regional enters a liquidity crisis or severe credit spiral, independent turnarounds are statistically rare. Usually acquired at discount or seized. Unlike a retailer that can close stores, a bank cannot pause operations without triggering a run. NYCB (2024) required vulture capital (Mnuchin), not organic operational fixes. However, for credit-cycle turnarounds (not liquidity crises), precedent is moderate (RF, SNV, ZION, CFG).

### Q4: Are outcomes typically non-binary?
**No** — Due to FDIC receivership mechanism, a bank is either open or closed on Friday night. Rarely a "slow decline" option. SVB and Silvergate went to zero virtually overnight. Contrasts with REITs that might trade at 0.5x book for years while working out assets.

### Q5: Is macro/geopolitical exposure limited?
**Yes** — Almost entirely domestic. Minimal FX, sanctions, or cross-border exposure. "Macro" risk limited to US GDP, unemployment, and Fed policy. Regional banks like HBAN or KEY were immune to Russia-Ukraine or China RE crisis direct impacts.

## Epistemic Stability

**Rating: 2/5 (Low Stability)**

Compared to G-SIBs (4/5 — TBTF, diversified revenue, operational deposits), regionals suffer the "Glass Cannon" problem:
1. Transparency is a liability during panic — investors can instantly calculate HTM losses
2. CRE concentration adds structural overhang without revenue diversification to absorb
3. 2023 proved deposit stickiness is a myth in digital age — runs now take hours not weeks

## PCS Friction

**Suggested: -2**

Rationale: Regulatory uncertainty (Basel III Endgame rewriting rules) + liquidity unpredictability (digital runs) + binary equity outcome risk (seizure). Regional bank equity is effectively a leveraged option on the yield curve.

*Note: Gemini's -2 recommendation may be overly conservative. Cross-reference against diversified banks at -1. A -1 default with -2 for banks with CRE >250%, uninsured deposits >50%, or unrealized losses >15% of TBV may be more calibrated.*
