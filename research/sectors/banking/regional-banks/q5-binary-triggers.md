## Balance Sheet & Financial Structure Risks

| Trigger | Frequency | Mechanism | Historical Example | Outcome |
|---------|-----------|-----------|-------------------|---------|
| Deposit Runs (uninsured concentration) | Occasional (cyclical) | >50% uninsured deposits. Solvency questioned, uninsured flee instantly. Cash buffers drain in hours. | SVB (2023) — 94% uninsured, $42B in one day | Failure/Seizure |
| CRE Concentration Blow-up | Structural | High CRE exposure. Vacancies rise + refi rates spike, LTVs invert. Reserves fail to cover. | Bank of New England (1991); NYCB (2024) stock -60% | Failure (BNE); Emergency recap (NYCB) |
| Interest Rate Mismatch (HTM losses) | Structural (rate cycle) | Long-duration bonds at low yield. Rates rise, bonds lose value. Forced sale crystallizes losses. | First Republic (2023) — $100B+ hole | Failure/Sale |
| Regulatory Seizure (PCA triggers) | Rare | CET1 breaches "Critically Undercapitalized" (<2%). Mandatory FDIC seizure. | IndyMac (2008) — seized Friday after capital collapse | Failure/Seizure |
| Credit Rating Downgrade Spiral | Occasional | Downgrade to junk triggers collateral calls, blocks Fed discount window. Drains remaining cash. | First Republic (2023) — downgrades accelerated failure | Accelerated Failure |

| Trigger | Frequency | Mechanism | Historical Example | Outcome |

## Frequency Summary
- Rare: Fraud, Cyber, Regulatory Seizure (require criminal negligence or black swan)
- Occasional: Deposit Runs, Rating Downgrades, Contagion (surface during contractions/hiking cycles)
- Structural: CRE Concentration, HTM/Rate Mismatch, Social Media speed (permanent vulnerabilities)
