## Key Findings

### Average Recovery Timeline
- Balance sheet repair: 3-5 years
- Stock recovery leads fundamentals by 12-18 months
- Crisis of confidence (liquidity): 6-18 months acute phase

### 20-Year Independent Survivorship Rate
- <40% for distressed regionals
- Most get acquired in "take-under" transactions
- 2004: ~9,000 FDIC-insured institutions. Today: <4,600.

### Regional vs Diversified Bank Turnarounds
1. Lack of fee income levers — regionals are NIM-dependent, can't offset with trading/advisory
2. Geographic concentration — must diversify away from home market, which is their core advantage
3. Regulation as hurdle — post-GFC/2023 compliance costs hit regionals disproportionately (can't amortize over G-SIB scale)

### M&A as Turnaround Exit
1. "Take-under" (failure prevention): forced sale at discount to avoid receivership (National City to PNC, FRC to JPM)
2. "Scale play" (post-recovery): merge for ROE-viable scale (CIT + First Citizens)
3. "Divestiture" (capital generation): sell non-core assets for non-dilutive capital (Regions selling Morgan Keegan)
