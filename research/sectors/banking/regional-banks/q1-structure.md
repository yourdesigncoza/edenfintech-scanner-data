## Revenue Model
- Primarily NII: collect deposits, make loans (commercial, CRE, residential mortgage, consumer)
- Non-interest income: ~20-30% (service charges, mortgage banking fees, wealth management)
- Community bank NIM Q4 2024: 3.44% (+9bps QoQ); below pre-pandemic avg of 3.63%
- Large banks NIM: 2.5-3.5%; community/regional banks: 3.5-4.5% (typically higher)
- NIM compression: deposit repricing lagged asset repricing during rate hike cycle 2022-2023

## Deposit Franchise
- Core retail/commercial deposits = primary funding source
- Deposit stickiness varies: operational deposits more sticky than brokered/uninsured
- March 2023 demonstrated risk: 85% uninsured deposits at SVB enabled $42B run in one day
- Target: <25% uninsured deposits as prudent threshold

## CRE Exposure
- 31% of all banks CRE-concentrated at YE 2024 (1,374 banks)
- Total CRE loans: ~$3T at end of 2024
- Regional banks: for $100 in asset growth, put $37.30 toward CRE vs $3.32 at large banks
- Office CRE: $626B maturing by end 2025 (as of search date)
- Source: Wharton paper "Too-Many-to-Ignore: Regional Banks and CRE Risks"

## Competitive Advantages
- Relationship banking in local markets
- Lower overhead vs mega-banks (nimbler technology adoption)
- Higher NIM vs mega-banks (less cross-subsidization of low-margin lines)
