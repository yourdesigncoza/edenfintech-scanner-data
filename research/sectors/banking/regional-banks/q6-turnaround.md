## Synovus Financial (SNV) — 2009-2015 Recovery
- Distress trigger: Real estate concentration losses in GFC; NPLs exceeded 5% threshold (Aug 2009 — listed as one of 150+ US lenders with NPLs ≥5% threatening survival)
- Capital raises: raised capital twice; last major bank to repay TARP (owed more than any other bank per search)
- Recovery actions: consolidated 30 separate banking charters into one (2010), new CEO Kessel Stelling (2012), balance sheet cleanup, diversified into commercial banking + wealth management
- Timeline: ~3-4 years from trough (2009) to operational stability; stock recovery to pre-crisis levels ~6-7 years
- Outcome: Emerged stronger; now considered M&A candidate (Pinnacle + Synovus merger announced per search)
- Source: https://www.bankdirector.com/article/building-a-powerhouse-at-synovus/; Wikipedia

## Washington Mutual (WMU) — 2008 FAILURE (Non-Recovery)
- Largest bank failure in US history ($300B thrift)
- Trigger: High Alt-A/subprime mortgage concentration; depositor run ($16.7B in 10 days)
- Outcome: FDIC-managed sale to JPMorgan Chase — no equity recovery
- Kill factor example: uninsured deposit concentration + asset quality failure = binary wipeout
- Source: https://www.fdic.gov/news/speeches/2023/spaug1423.html

## First Republic Bank (FRC) — 2023 FAILURE (Non-Recovery)
- Trigger: SVB contagion panic + underlying problem: $100B+ uninsured deposits + HTM portfolio losses
- Run: $70B deposit outflow Q1 2023
- Outcome: FDIC receivership May 2023; JPMorgan acquired assets/deposits
- Kill factor: high uninsured deposit share (68%) + duration mismatch = liquidity binary
- Source: https://www.fdic.gov/news/speeches/2024/lessons-learned-us-regional-bank-failures-2023

## Pattern — Recoverable vs Non-Recoverable
- Recoverable: Credit losses only (NPL spike), capital raised in time, new management installed, regulatory actions stayed short of conservatorship
- Non-recoverable: Liquidity run triggered (uninsured deposit flight), especially when combined with unrealized HTM losses that crystallize during fire-sale
- Key distinction: Solvency crisis (can raise capital, long runway) vs Liquidity crisis (hours to days, binary)

## Typical Recovery Timeline
- Operational distress recovery: 3-5 years from trough to pre-crisis profitability
- Regulatory enforcement action: 2-4 years to close
- Stock recovery after distress (successful): 4-7 years to prior highs
