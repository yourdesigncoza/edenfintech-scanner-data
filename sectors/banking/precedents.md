## Cross-Sector Patterns

### What Successful Bank Turnarounds Have in Common

1. **New CEO with mandate to simplify** — BAC (Moynihan), C (Fraser), WFC (Scharf), SNV (charter consolidation). The turnaround agent is always a person with authority to restructure.

2. **Decisive asset disposition** — Selling bad loans/assets at a loss NOW rather than slow-walking write-downs over years. RF (Morgan Keegan), SNV (bulk distressed sales), ZION (toxic CDOs).

3. **Capital raise early in the process** — Dilutive but survivable. SNV ($1.1B), NCC tried ($7B) but too late. KEY (Scotiabank $2.8B — strategic, not dilutive).

4. **Regulatory resolution as prerequisite** — No bank re-rates while consent orders are outstanding. WFC re-rated only after asset cap removal (7+ years). C still working through consent orders.

5. **Multi-year timelines are the norm** — Bank turnarounds: 3-7 years for regionals, 7-15 years for diversified. Not 2-3 years. This challenges the 30% CAGR hurdle unless entry is near absolute trough.

### What Distinguishes Successful from Failed

| Factor | Successful | Failed |
|--------|-----------|--------|
| Speed of crisis | Gradual credit deterioration | Binary liquidity crisis |
| Capital raise timing | Early, proactive | Late, reactive (too dilutive or too slow) |
| Deposit base | Primarily insured, sticky | Primarily uninsured, concentrated |
| Asset quality | Impaired but identifiable | Hidden (fraud, HTM losses) |
| Regulatory relationship | Cooperative, working toward resolution | Adversarial, or regulator loses confidence |
| Business model | Diversified enough to survive stress | Concentrated (single sector, geography, or depositor type) |

### Recovery Timelines by Type

| Crisis Type | Typical Timeline | CAGR Implication |
|------------|-----------------|-----------------|
| Credit cycle (GFC-style) | 4-7 years for regionals, 10-15 for diversified | 15-25% CAGR if entered at trough |
| Regulatory cleanup | 3-7 years to clear consent orders | 10-20% CAGR (slow re-rating) |
| Contagion/confidence | 6-18 months acute, 2-3 years full | 30%+ CAGR possible if fundamentals intact |
| Structural (CRE, secular) | 5-10+ years | <15% CAGR — likely fails hurdle rate |

### M&A as Exit Path

M&A serves three roles in bank turnarounds:
1. **Take-under (failure prevention):** Forced sale at discount to avoid receivership. NCC to PNC ($2.23), FRC to JPM.
2. **Scale play (post-recovery):** Merge for ROE-viable scale due to compliance costs. CIT + First Citizens.
3. **Divestiture (capital generation):** Sell non-core assets for non-dilutive capital. RF selling Morgan Keegan, KEY selling insurance units.

**For EdenFinTech:** M&A provides a valuation floor for regional banks with attractive deposit franchises. Well-run regionals in growing geographies rarely sustain P/TBV below 1.0x for extended periods — acquirers step in. However, relying on M&A as the thesis catalyst is weak because timing is unpredictable and premiums for distressed banks are typically modest (1.0-1.5x TBV vs 1.5-2.0x for healthy franchises).
