# Consumer Defensive -- Valuation Guide

## Valuation Guidelines Reference

Per `valuation-guidelines.md`, Consumer Staples baseline FCF multiple is **25-28x**. This applies to durable branded companies with stable cash flows. Discounts apply cumulatively per the discount schedule.

## Sub-Sector Valuation Approaches

### Beverages - Alcoholic (Brewers)

| Method | Multiple Range | When to Use |
|--------|---------------|-------------|
| EV/EBITDA | 10-14x (large-cap) | Primary -- normalizes for capital structure, seasonal working capital |
| P/FCF | 15-22x | Secondary -- for stable brewers with consistent capex |
| P/E | 12-18x | Mega-cap (AB InBev, Heineken) with stable structures |

**Where standard FCF multiples DON'T fully apply:** Large brewers carry significant goodwill from M&A history. Goodwill impairments (Molson Coors $3.65B in Q3 2025) distort earnings. Use EV/EBITDA as primary and cross-check with P/FCF.

### Beverages - Wineries & Distilleries

| Method | Multiple Range | When to Use |
|--------|---------------|-------------|
| EV/EBITDA | 10-14x (premium); 8-12x (wine); 6-9x (value) | Primary -- adjusts for capital structure and inventory distortions |
| EV/Revenue | 2.0-4.0x (premium brands); 1.0-2.0x (mainstream) | M&A comparables; high-growth premium brands |
| P/E | 15-25x (premium spirits) | Brown-Forman historically 20x+, now ~16x after de-rating |

**Where standard FCF multiples DON'T apply:** Aging inventory (barrel stock for spirits aged 2-12+ years) creates massive working capital requirements that depress FCF relative to EBITDA. A distiller building bourbon barrel stock has legitimately negative FCF during build-out without being distressed. ALWAYS adjust for inventory build when using FCF-based multiples.

### Packaged Foods

| Method | Multiple Range | When to Use |
|--------|---------------|-------------|
| P/FCF | 18-25x (branded); 12-16x (PL manufacturer) | Primary for stable branded companies -- best captures durability premium |
| EV/EBITDA | 10-14x (branded); 7-10x (PL mfg) | Cross-company comparability; M&A benchmarks |
| EV/Revenue | 1.5-3.0x (branded); 0.8-1.5x (PL mfg) | Distressed situations where margins are temporarily depressed |

**Standard FCF multiples apply well.** Packaged foods companies have predictable capex, stable working capital, and consistent FCF generation. Apply the discount schedule from the baseline 25-28x per valuation guidelines.

### Household & Personal Products

| Method | Multiple Range | When to Use |
|--------|---------------|-------------|
| P/FCF | 20-28x (category leaders); 15-22x (mid-cap niche); 10-16x (restructuring) | Primary -- HPC has among the most predictable FCF profiles in all of consumer staples |
| EV/EBITDA | 14-18x (leaders); 10-14x (mid-cap); 7-10x (distressed) | Cross-company comparability |
| P/E | 20-30x (P&G/Colgate-class); 12-18x (mid-cap) | Stable mega-caps with consistent capital structures |

**Standard FCF multiples apply well.** Low capital intensity, predictable demand, strong cash conversion. P&G benchmark (25-30x P/FCF) is NOT available to mid-cap competitors -- do not apply P&G multiples to Edgewell or Spectrum Brands.

## Discount Schedule Application

Per valuation-guidelines.md, apply these discounts cumulatively from the Consumer Staples baseline (25-28x P/FCF):

| Condition | Discount | Most Affected Sub-Sectors |
|-----------|----------|--------------------------|
| Revenue/volume declining 2+ consecutive years | -3x to -5x | All (especially beverages, wine) |
| Above-average leverage vs peers | -2x to -4x | Mid-cap food, leveraged acquirers |
| Regulatory, geopolitical, or structural risk | -2x to -3x | Beverages (tariffs), wine (secular decline) |
| Limited pricing power (commodity product, PL pressure) | -1x to -3x | Packaged foods (PL exposed), HPC (commoditized categories) |
| Secular industry headwind | -2x to -4x | Wine (consumption decline), beer (volume decline), processed food (health trends) |

**Example application:** A packaged food company (baseline 25-28x P/FCF) with declining volume (-4x), rising PL competition (-3x), and above-average leverage (-3x) = 15-18x P/FCF. Equivalent to roughly 8-10x EV/EBITDA.

## M&A Transaction Benchmarks (2024-2025)

| Transaction | Date | EV/EBITDA | EV/Revenue | Context |
|------------|------|-----------|------------|---------|
| Carlsberg / Britvic | July 2024 | 14.8x | 2.1x | Premium beverage brand |
| Celsius / Alani Nutrition | Feb 2025 | 13.8x | 3.2x | High-growth functional beverage |
| Smucker's / Hostess Brands | Nov 2023 | ~20x | ~4.5x | Iconic snack brands with asset-light model |
| Investindustrial / TreeHouse Foods | Nov 2025 | ~10x | ~0.9x | Private label manufacturer; take-private |

**Key insight from M&A:** The spread between premium/growth brands (13-20x EV/EBITDA) and value/declining brands (8-10x) is widening. Quality and scarcity command premiums. Distressed assets trade at significant discounts.

## Consistency Rule Reminder

Per valuation-guidelines.md: Within a single scan, no candidate's FCF multiple may deviate by more than 5x from the scan's median multiple without explicit written justification. If a scan includes both a premium branded company (20x+ P/FCF) and a distressed value company (12x P/FCF), the 5x consistency check requires justification.

## Heroic Assumptions Test

A valuation is "heroic" if ANY of:
- Revenue growth required is 3x the industry average (industry avg: 1-3% for consumer staples)
- FCF margin estimate exceeds the company's 10-year peak
- FCF multiple exceeds the company's historical median by more than 50%

For consumer staples specifically: watch for analysts projecting 8-10% revenue growth in a 2-3% growth category, or assuming margin recovery to levels the company achieved only briefly during a pricing cycle.
